## The Strategic Narrative

Most email platforms force SaaS teams into an impossible choice: pick a developer tool with a great API but no way for your marketing/product team to touch anything (Resend, Postmark) — or pick a marketing platform that your dev team hates integrating with (Mailchimp, SendGrid).

Lettr eliminates this choice. It's a clean API that developers actually enjoy working with, connected to a best-in-class visual editor that marketing and product people can use independently. Developers set up the workflows. The rest of the team owns the content. Nobody files a Jira ticket to change a button color.

And because Lettr is built exclusively for SaaS — no ecommerce integrations, no Shopify plugins, no bloat — every feature, template, and metric is designed for the emails SaaS companies actually send.

## Target Audience

### Primary buyer: The Developer (often a founder or tech lead)

- Works at a small-to-medium SaaS company (2–50 people)
- Has been burned by bloated email platforms before
- Wants to integrate email once and never think about infrastructure again
- Cares about: clean API, good docs, fast integration, fair pricing, reliability
- Emotional driver: "I want one tool for transactional AND marketing emails so I don't manage two vendors, and I never want my non-technical cofounder pinging me about email changes."

### Secondary user: The Product/Marketing Person

- Joins after the developer has already integrated Lettr
- Needs to edit email content, manage templates, build campaigns
- Does NOT want to write code or depend on developers for every change
- Cares about: drag-and-drop editing, preview, version control, analytics
- Emotional driver: "I can actually do my job without a dev ticket."

### Buying motion

Developer-led. Dev puts it on the company card. No procurement, no sales calls needed. Free tier → paid as they grow. Small SaaS companies will grow with Lettr.

## Competitive Positioning

### vs. Resend

Resend has a great developer experience but essentially no marketer-facing tools. If your product/marketing team needs to edit templates or run campaigns, they're stuck. Lettr gives you the same clean API PLUS a full drag-and-drop editor (powered by Topol, used by 40K+ companies) so your whole team is unblocked.

### vs. Postmark

Postmark is reliable but expensive and transactional-only. No campaign features, limited template editing. Lettr covers both transactional and marketing emails in one platform, with better data retention and more competitive pricing.

### vs. SendGrid / Mailchimp

Over-engineered, expensive, painful to integrate, designed for a different era. Lettr is purpose-built for modern SaaS teams — not retrofitted from a marketing automation platform.

### vs. Sidemail

Closest positioning overlap. Sidemail also targets SaaS and offers both transactional + marketing. Lettr's advantages: the Topol editor quality (best-in-class drag-and-drop, synced sections, multilingual templates), infrastructure heritage from Ecomail, and more aggressive pricing at scale.

## Key Differentiators (ranked by importance)

1. **Dual-audience by design** — Dev-friendly API + best-in-class visual editor for non-technical teammates. Not bolted on, built in from day one.

2. **Built exclusively for SaaS** — No ecommerce integrations, no Shopify bloat. Every feature, template, and metric serves SaaS use cases: onboarding flows, trial expiry, password resets, usage alerts, product updates, changelog digests, feature announcements.

3. **Transactional + marketing in one platform** — No more managing two vendors. Send password resets AND product newsletters from the same place, on the same domain, with the same reputation.

4. **Topol editor inside** — The same drag-and-drop editor trusted by 40,000+ companies. Synced sections, multilingual templates, version history, draft/publish workflow. This is not a basic template editor — it's genuinely best-in-class.

5. **Enterprise-grade infrastructure, startup-friendly pricing** — Backed by Ecomail's 12-year-old infrastructure serving 12,000+ organizations. But starting at $0/mo for 3,000 emails, $15/mo for 50,000.

6. **First-party domain control** — Custom tracking domains and hosted asset domains from day one. Rare among competitors at this price point.

## Messaging Do's and Don'ts

### Do:

- Lead with the dual-audience value (dev + team)
- Use SaaS-specific examples and language throughout
- Show real code snippets (devs want to see the API)
- Show the visual editor (marketers want to see it's real)
- Mention the Topol and Ecomail heritage as credibility, not as the main story
- Keep the tone confident but not arrogant — you're the focused alternative, not the incumbent

### Don't:

- Say "for Artisans" — too Laravel-specific, alienates non-Laravel devs
- Lead with "transactional email" — too narrow now that campaigns are coming
- Position against specific competitors by name on the homepage
- Overpromise on the campaign/audience features before they ship
- Make it sound like a sub-product of Topol or Ecomail — Lettr stands on its own
- Use generic email marketing language ("grow your list," "boost engagement") — this is SaaS, not DTC
